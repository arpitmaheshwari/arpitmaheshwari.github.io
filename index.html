MY DESIGN
PHILOSOPHY

What guides my work
01

Empathy before pixels

Every design decision begins with a person — their goals, frustrations, and mental models. I spend more time listening than sketching. Understanding comes first; solutions follow. A solution without empathy is just decoration.

02

Simplicity is earned, not given

Simple interfaces are the hardest to design. They require deep understanding of the problem, the courage to remove what doesn't serve the user, and the discipline to resist over-engineering. Complexity is easy. Clarity is the work.

03

Ship, learn, evolve

Products are never finished — they're grown. I believe in launching early with intent, observing real behaviour, and iterating based on evidence. A product shipped and refined beats a perfect product never launched.

04

Accessibility is not optional

Designing for the margins improves the experience for everyone. Inclusive design isn't a constraint — it's a creative challenge that makes better products. Every user deserves a dignified experience.

05

Design must speak business

Beautiful design that doesn't move the needle is a vanity project. I measure success in outcomes — adoption, retention, task completion, revenue impact. Good design and good business are not in tension; they're inseparable.

06

Collaboration multiplies ideas

I work fluidly with engineers, product managers, data scientists, and business stakeholders. The best design decisions emerge from honest cross-functional tension — not from a designer working in isolation and handing things over a wall.
