My Design
Philosophy

What guides my work

01
Empathy Before Pixels
Every design decision begins with a person — their goals, frustrations, and mental models. I spend more time listening than sketching. Understanding comes first; solutions follow. A solution without empathy is just decoration.
02
Simplicity is Earned, Not Given
Simple interfaces are the hardest to design. They require deep understanding of the problem, the courage to remove what doesn't serve the user, and the discipline to resist over-engineering. Complexity is easy. Clarity is the work.
03
Ship, Learn, Evolve
AI products are never finished — they learn and grow. I believe in launching early, observing real behaviour alongside model performance, and iterating based on both human feedback and data evidence.
04
Accessibility is Not Optional
AI-powered products must be usable by everyone. Designing for the margins improves the experience for all. Inclusive design isn't a constraint — it's a creative challenge that makes intelligent products better.
05
AI Must Be Explainable
If a user can't understand why an AI made a recommendation, the design has failed. I design for transparency — surfacing confidence levels, data provenance, and clear fallback paths so users stay in control.
06
Human + Machine, Not Either/Or
The best AI products augment human judgement rather than replacing it. I design collaboration patterns between people and algorithms — keeping humans in the loop where it matters most.

My Process

01

Discover & Research

Understand the problem space through user interviews, stakeholder workshops, competitor audits, and data analysis before touching any design tools.

02

Frame & Ideate

Define the problem clearly, then use design frameworks to ideate multiple solutions. Divergent thinking before convergent clarity.

03

Prototype & Test

Test ideas with quick lo-fi prototypes. Fail fast, learn faster. Usability tests with real users refine solutions before build begins.

04

Launch & Evolve

Ship, measure, iterate. Design doesn't end at handoff — I stay engaged through launch and feedback cycles to evolve the product over time.
