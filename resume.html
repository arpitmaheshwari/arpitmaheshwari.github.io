Design Philosophy (What I Believe)
01. Understand Before You Design I start with the data, not the screen. Before opening Figma, I sit with engineers to understand model behavior, edge cases, and failure modes.
02. Make the Complex Feel Obvious I design for the person who doesn't speak "ML" — translating algorithmic outputs into decisions, not data dumps.
03. Show the Why, Not Just the What Users don't trust black boxes. When AI recommends something, I design explainability into the interface — transparency is the foundation of trust.
04. Design the Failure State First AI is wrong sometimes. I design what happens when models are uncertain or data is missing. Users forgive errors; they don't forgive confusion.
05. Accessible by Default An AI dashboard that only works for sighted mouse users fails half its audience. I build WCAG compliance into the first sprint, not the last.
