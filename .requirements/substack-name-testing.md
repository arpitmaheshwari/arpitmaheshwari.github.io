# Substack Name Testing — Creative Clarity
**Test Date:** 2026-04-21  
**Candidate:** Creative Clarity

---

## Test 1: Google Search & Discoverability

**Simulate:** What would people search for?
- "creative clarity newsletter" — Generic, moderate competition
- "design clarity" — Better, but broad
- "creative clarity design" — Good, specific niche
- "AI product design newsletter" — Actually more direct

**SEO Strength:** Medium  
**Verdict:** Not optimized for your specific keywords (AI, data-intensive, product design)

---

## Test 2: Brand Clarity (Does it communicate what you do?)

**Show someone:** "Creative Clarity — newsletter by Arpit Maheshwari"

**What do they think?**
- Designer talking about being creative + clear? ✅
- Specifically about AI/data products? ❌ (Not obvious)
- About designing intelligent systems? ❓ (Unclear)

**Verdict:** Elegant but vague. Doesn't immediately signal "AI product design"

---

## Test 3: Differentiation (Is it unique?)

**Search "Creative Clarity" on Substack:**

**Verdict:** Need to check — could be generic enough others use it

## Key Findings

### Strengths of "Creative Clarity"
✅ More memorable + branded than "Data-Intensive Thinking"  
✅ Sophisticated double meaning (like Signals/Threshold)  
✅ Works across social/casual mention  
✅ Professional, distinctive  

### Weaknesses of "Creative Clarity"
❌ Doesn't explicitly signal AI/data focus (unlike current name)  
❌ Less SEO-optimized for your target keywords  
❌ Relies on existing audience knowing your work  
❌ Generic enough others might use it  

---

## Questions to Resolve

1. **Do you prioritize:** Brand distinctiveness OR keyword optimization?
   - If distinctiveness → Creative Clarity wins
   - If SEO/clarity → Data-Intensive Thinking or AI-specific name wins

2. **How will people find you?**
   - Through Google search for AI/design? (Need keywords in name)
   - Through personal network/Twitter? (Creative Clarity works fine)
   - Through LinkedIn recommendations? (Name matters less)

3. **Long-term brand vision?**
   - Generic "design thinking" brand? (Creative Clarity works)
   - Specific authority in AI/data design? (Need specificity in name)

---

## Recommendation Options

### Option A: Go with "Creative Clarity"
**If:** You have strong audience + willing to rely on personal discovery (Twitter, LinkedIn, referrals)  
**Trade-off:** Sacrifice SEO potential for brand sophistication

### Option B: Hybrid Approach
Combine both qualities:
- **Clarity by Design** (adds design specificity)
- **AI Clarity** (adds AI keyword, less elegant)
- **Design Clarity** (adds design keyword, simpler)

### Option C: Keyword-First Name
Prioritize SEO + clarity:
- **Designing for AI** (direct, searchable, clear)
- **AI Interface Design** (very specific, less branded)
- **Intelligent Design** (broad, memorable, okay SEO)

## How to Test Further

**Before deciding, try this:**

1. **Google test:** Search "creative clarity newsletter" — what appears?
2. **Audience test:** Mention "I'm launching Creative Clarity" to 3-5 people in your network — what's their first impression?
3. **Availability test:** Check Substack, Twitter handle, domain — is it available/unique?
4. **30-day test:** Pick a name, commit for 30 days, see if it feels right after real usage
