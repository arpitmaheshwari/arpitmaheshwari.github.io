Telefónica MyO2 & Priority Moments

Telecom · O2 UK (Telefónica) · Designed & Built · Mobile Web · 2013–14

Designed and built. Not designed, then handed off. Every screen of both products — MyO2 and Priority Moments — on mobile web, at national scale. 4M+ users. 2.6M sign-ups in year one.

Designer and front-end · Delivered through Equal Experts

Role: Designer and front-end engineer · Designed and built every screen on mobile web · On contract through Equal Experts · O2 UK (Telefónica) · MyO2 2014 · Priority Moments 2013

MyO2 — put the account in the customer's hands.

MyO2 (2014) is O2 UK's official self-service app — the one place a customer can check their data, calls, and texts, view and pay a bill, change tariff, see upgrade options, and manage the whole account without phoning anyone. The business case was simple: every routine task a customer can do for themselves is a call the contact centre never has to take. The job was to make those everyday account tasks genuinely self-serve.

I designed and then built every screen on mobile web — first as the designer working out how usage, billing, and tariff changes should look and flow, then as the front-end engineer turning that design into the production code customers actually used. I worked on contract through Equal Experts, the consultancy O2 brought in to build the product.

MyO2 went on to serve more than 4 million users. I designed and built the screens those millions of self-service journeys ran on.

Priority Moments — turn a phone bill into a reason to open the app.

O2 Priority Moments (2013) is O2's customer-loyalty programme: exclusive rewards and offers from high-street brands — Odeon, M&S, Caffè Nero and more — delivered through the app and mobile web, and matched to a customer using their interests, behaviour, and location. The point was to make "I'm an O2 customer" worth something every week, not just on the day the bill arrives.

It launched in July 2011 behind a £6m national campaign and hit 2.6 million registrations in year one. The app reached 2.5 million-plus active users and was the only loyalty programme carrying a 5-star App Store rating. As with MyO2, I designed and built the consumer-facing reward and offer screens on mobile web, on contract through Equal Experts.

The design problem here was different from MyO2. MyO2 is a utility — get in, do the task, get out. Priority Moments is a habit — the screen has to make a coffee or a cinema ticket feel like a small, well-earned win, surfaced at the right moment, without burying it in fine print. Two products, two completely different jobs for the same set of screens to do.

Designed it, then built it — on the device people actually carry.

On both products I sat on both sides of the handoff. I designed the screens and then wrote the front-end code that shipped them — so the thing a customer tapped was the thing I'd drawn, with nothing lost in translation between a design file and an engineer who'd never seen the intent behind it.

Everything was mobile web, at national scale, under one of the UK's biggest carrier brands — which means small things stop being small. A loading state, a tap target, a billing number that has to be exactly right: at millions of users, every one of those decisions is being made for a stadium-sized crowd at once. Both products were delivered for O2 through Equal Experts, the consultancy O2 brought in and that publicly states it built MyO2 and Priority Moments for the carrier.

The numbers on this page are the public outcomes of the products — reported by O2 and by Equal Experts — not private performance figures I'm claiming as my own. What I'm claiming is the work: I designed and built the screens those outcomes happened on.
